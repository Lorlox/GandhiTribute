"Be the change you wish to see in the world"- Gandhi

Here's a timeline of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - Born October 2nd at Portbandar, India
  • 1876 - Moves to Rajkot, India
  • 1883 - Marries wife, Kasturba, at age 13.
  • 1888 - Moves to England to study law.
  • 1891 - Returns to India to practice law.
  • 1893 - Accepts a job in South Africa
  • 1903 - Opens a law office in Johannesberg, South Africa
  • 1906 - Organizes first protest in South Africa against anti-Indian laws.
  • 1908 - Imprisoned in South Africa.
  • 1915 - Returns to India.
  • 1922 - Sentenced to six years in jail following trial.
  • 1930 - Jailed for breaking India's Salt Laws.
  • 1932 - Fasts to protest treatment of India's Untouchables.
  • 1984 - Launches All Indian Village Industries Association.
  • 1942 - Begins the "Quit India" movement.
  • 1944 - Kasturba, Gandhi's wife, dies at age 74.
  • 1947 - India gains independence from Britain.
  • 1947 - Gandhi is assassinated on January 30, at age 78 during a prayer meeting.

As a man changes his own nature, so does the attitude of the world change towards him. This is the divine mystery supreme. A wonderful thing it is and the source of our happiness. We need not wait to see what others do.”
